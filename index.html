Why It Cannot Be Replicated

Three Reasons This Asset
Is Truly One of a Kind

The Costa del Sol has changed. Dramatically. What was possible 20 years ago — assembling this much prime land, this close to the coast, with these planning permissions — is no longer possible today. For any padel operator looking to build a flagship destination on this coast, the question is not "should we build it?" — it is "where can we build it?" The answer is: only here.

01
Planning Restrictions

Coastal planning laws and environmental protection zones introduced across Andalucía make it legally impossible today to obtain equivalent permissions for a new-build sports estate of this size and proximity to the coast.

02
Land Scarcity

77,760 m² of contiguous, titled land in the Estepona–Marbella corridor simply does not come to market. Land of this scale, this close to the N-340 and the sea, is an extinct asset class on the Costa del Sol.

03
Built Infrastructure Value

The cost to build 19,154 m² of indoor/outdoor sports infrastructure today would far exceed the asking price — before even acquiring the land. The buyer inherits decades of investment at a fraction of replacement cost.
